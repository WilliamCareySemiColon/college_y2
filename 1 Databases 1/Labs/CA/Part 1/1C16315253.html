Decisions for Stock_Item

The first entity to be dealt with is the Stock Item. What this means is what items are in store to purchase. When running a programme to keep track of the stock items, what is needed to distinush any item from another is six attributes. The most important is the five digit unique identity code. This would allow the user of the programme to know instantly what the item is. There will be other details including the item type, how much it was purchased from the supplier (import_price), how much it will be sold for (export_price), how many in store (quatity) and the description behind the item. This would be, example a game console, what the speed of the console is and how to operate it on some level. It would also have the processor speed and what type of machines are needed to effectly run the console.

Relationships with other entities

It has a many to many relationship with supplier entity and one to many relationship with game entity. The reason behind these entities is one factor behind the two. That is how many can each have between the relationship. With supplier entity, stock can have many suppliers and the supplier can have many supplies of stock. With the game entity, stock can have many games but game can only be of one stock (type). The game would share the same ID code as one of the stock item due to the fact it is essentially part of the stock items itself.

Decisions for Games

There are two main features of games wich makes the unique. Their offical title and their stock Id code. These two features can be combine to make an even more unique item. However, this is not nesscary. The other features on the game itself is the consoles needed (could be more than one), the release date, age group and tthe required skills to play the game itself. We need to make sure the naming convection of the game itself is apporiate to the target audience. Once that is completed, the item itself will have a description (under the relationship with stock item). Included in this desciption is the skills required to play the game.

Relationships with other entities

It has a many to many relationship with Reviewer entity and many to one relationship with stock_item entity. Reviewers can review mutliple games and one game can have many reviewers. One stock can have many games but one game cannot be many stock.

Decisions for Supplier

The supplier itself is a company, therefore its name would be unique. Their main email and the id code assigned to them are both unique, as to effectly allow them to stand out. This would make three unique features, allowing the build of a onumious unique code. Their phone number, sales representive and stock supplied could be competed by other companies, making the other features not unique. Therfore the company name, their main email and company code are their primary keys. Their code must be of five digits and their email must have an '@' in it.

Relationships with other entities

It has a many to many relantion with stock item and sale representives, as stock can be supplied by many suppliers and suppliers can supply many stock items. With sale representives, there can be many sale representive working for a supplier and and sale representive can work for more than one supplier. With supply order, it is one to many relantion as the supplier can supply more than one order but supply orders can be from one company only.

Decisions for Sale Representive

The sale representive is someone who a person works with for business reasons. The name, phone number and email of that sale representive may not be unique. However, each one has a five digit unique code, which can be used to track their services. Because of this, that would be the primary key.

Relationships with other entities

It has a one to many relantion with supply order and a many to many relantion with suppliers, as an order can come through one sale representive at a time but one sale representive can do many supply order. One supplier company can many sale representive and one sale representive can work for mutliple suppliers.

Decisions on supply order

Supply order are very unique, as not only do they have their own six unique code, but adopt the sale representive unique code, the companies unique code, the company name and their main email as part of their unique features (due to their many to one relantions). As a result of these, they are unique in many of their own ways. It takes other primary keys as unique foreign keys as well as adopting it own unique primary key.

Decisions on reviewers

Reviewers are only linked to the game sector, as that is all they can review. There is a many to many relation with games, as one reviewer can review more than one game, as well as one game can have many reviewers. The reviewers have a unique username and a list of the games they already have reviewed. This is due to the fact they cannot review the same twice. Because of this, the username and the review list of games are the primary keys.
